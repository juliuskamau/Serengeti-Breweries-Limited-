Skill of Life

Our Skills for Life initiative gives back to the communities under the education pillar through College/university scholarships. Since inception in 2001, EABL Foundationâ€™s Skills for Life programme has awarded over 200 university scholarships to bright and needy students across East Africa, with at least 30 of them from Uganda. Every year, scholarships are awarded to bright and needy students who, despite excelling in their studies, cannot afford university education. Successful scholars were receiving a complete university scholarship package inclusive of tuition, books, accommodation and a monthly stipend. The programme partners with both private and public universities in the region including Mzumbe University in Tanzania, Strathmore University in Kenya and Makerere University in Uganda.

Now that SBL is dependent on agricultural produce for sourcing its raw materials, there has been a need to shift these scholarships to be more strategically demand driven targeting students pursuing specialized courses in agricultural studies. In F18 SBL sourced 15K metric tons of grains locally equivalent to 80% of its annual raw materials requirement. The vision is to reach 85% in F20. SBLâ€™s Agribusiness that supports over 300 farmers in 8 regions plays a great role in promoting local sourcing. PROJECT KILIMO-VIWANDA

Project Kilimo-Viwanda is a program initiative to strengthen the exiting pool of agricultural experts in LRM growing regions. The program intends to provide scholarships to students from LRM communities pursuing agricultural based trainings in technical colleges under the ministry of agriculture. The ministry of agriculture is exclusively mandated to run 14 public agricultural institutions countrywide which provides certificate and diploma level courses in agri-related fields.

The sponsorship will be extended to at least 30 students annually pursuing diplomas in agricultural based courses. Beneficiaries of the program are expected to become field officers/lead advisors in the respective LRM communities providing the farmers with day to day technical to sustainably conduct their farming activities. The SBLâ€™s LRM potential growing regions are Arusha, Kilimanjaro, Manyara, Dodoma, lake regions, Singida and the southern highlands. All these are targeted by our scholarship program.
